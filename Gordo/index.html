Introduction to Gordo the monkey

Gordo was the ninth monkey that American scientists sent to space during the early years of U.S. and Soviet Union space race. The objective of his mission was to observe his life signs during the takeoff, landing and weightlessness.

Gordo was a squirrel monkey of South American descent and was chosen for this mission because of his human anatomical similarities as well as temperature sensitivities.He was trained to spend his time suited in a special type of space suit that would protect him from the vacuum of space, if the capsule were to become compromised. Surface of the suit was equipped with various monitoring devices witch transmitted their findings back to earth in real-time.

December 13, 1958

Gordo the Brave Monkey

The launch of his Jupiter AM-13 was executed in the early morning on December 13, 1958 at Cape Canaveral, Florida. During his 15 minute flight time, he traveled over 1500 miles, reaching altitudes of 310 miles and over 8 minutes of weightlessness. The scientists who monitored his life signs were pleased to see that he was reacting really well to the conditions in outer space.

Gordo's Legacy

Gordo, who was also known as Old Reliable, was sent into space in 1958. A squirrel monkey, he was chosen because of how similar his species was to the human body.

Gordo was lost on impact and neither him nor the shuttle was recovered; however, scientists were heartened by the mission because they believed it helped prove that humans could survive in space. Top of Page »

Thank you Gordo

What was gained in losing Gordo

The death of Gordo proved that space conditions did not change the life functions, and several more missions were commissioned to confirm those claims.

Although Gordo was lost, scientists believe that his life was not taken in vain. With the loss of this poor monkey, many important discoveries were made, leading to the end of animals being sent into space.Top of Page »
